Industry: Petrochemical

Products:
  - Name: Ethylene
    Type: Olefin
    Description: A colorless, flammable gas and one of the most important petrochemical feedstocks.
    Production:
      Method: Steam Cracking
      Feedstock:
        - Naphtha
        - Ethane
    Derivatives:
      - Polyethylene (PE)
      - Ethylene Oxide
      - Ethylene Glycol
      - PVC (via intermediates)
    Uses:
      - Plastic packaging (bags, films, bottles)
      - Antifreeze (via ethylene glycol)
      - Textile fibers (polyester)
      - Detergents and solvents
    Properties:
      - Highly reactive
      - Flammable gas
      - Lightweight molecule

  - Name: Propylene
    Type: Olefin
    Description: A key petrochemical used in producing a wide range of plastics and chemicals.
    Production:
      Method:
        - Steam Cracking
        - Fluid Catalytic Cracking (FCC)
      Feedstock:
        - Naphtha
        - Propane
    Derivatives:
      - Polypropylene (PP)
      - Acrylonitrile
      - Propylene Oxide
      - Cumene
    Uses:
      - Automotive parts (plastic components)
      - Packaging materials
      - Textiles (synthetic fibers)
      - Consumer goods
    Properties:
      - Flammable gas
      - Moderate reactivity
      - Versatile industrial use

Processes:
  - Name: Steam Cracking
    Description: High-temperature process used to break down hydrocarbons into smaller molecules like ethylene and propylene.

  - Name: Fluid Catalytic Cracking (FCC)
    Description: Refining process that converts heavy hydrocarbons into lighter products including propylene.

Applications:
  Plastics:
    - Packaging materials
    - Bottles and containers
    - Films and wraps

  Chemicals:
    - Solvents
    - Detergents
    - Industrial chemicals

  Industrial:
    - Automotive components
    - Construction materials
    - Electrical insulation

Safety:
  Guidelines:
    - Handle flammable gases with care
    - Use proper industrial storage systems
    - Follow environmental and safety regulations

Notes:
  - Ethylene and propylene are foundational building blocks of the petrochemical industry
  - Widely used across multiple sectors globally
  - Demand is closely tied to manufacturing and economic activity
